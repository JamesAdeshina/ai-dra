How it works

Three steps from camera to clinical insight

Survivor opens a session

The platform loads in any modern browser. No downloads, no wearables. The survivor selects their exercise and the camera activates — guided by simple onscreen instructions.

AI tracks the movement

MediaPipe analyses body and hand landmarks in real time — measuring joint angles, tracking rep completion, timing holds, and scoring movement quality, entirely within the browser.

Feedback and progress

Live prompts guide each movement. Sessions are saved automatically so survivors, carers, and clinicians can review progress, spot trends, and adjust the programme over time.

"Many stroke survivors lose access to therapy shortly after hospital discharge — AI-DRA is designed to continue that support safely at home."

Stroke is one of the leading causes of long-term disability in the UK. Community rehabilitation services are limited and difficult to reach, leaving survivors without the continued support they need to maintain progress.

AI-DRA is a seed-funded research project at the University of Derby, building a prototype that combines computer vision, machine learning, and accessible design to deliver guided upper-limb rehabilitation wherever the survivor is.

Platform features

Built for survivors, carers, and clinicians

Real-time movement tracking

Joint angles, rep counts, hold timers, and movement stage detection — updated live using your device camera, with no data leaving the browser during tracking.

AI-guided coaching

Spoken and on-screen prompts adapt to each movement — telling the survivor when to raise, hold, or return, and flagging when form needs adjustment.

Carer and clinician access

Carers can view linked survivors' progress with permission. Clinicians and researchers can monitor engagement, session quality, and outcomes through a dedicated dashboard.

Longitudinal progress tracking

Every session is stored — reps completed, movement scores, hold durations, and exercise history — so recovery progress is visible over days, weeks, and months.
